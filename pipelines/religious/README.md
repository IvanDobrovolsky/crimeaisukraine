# Religious Organizations: Legitimizing Sovereignty Through the Sacred

**Headline:** The Moscow Patriarchate annexed three Ukrainian dioceses in Crimea (June 2022), the largest Orthodox schism since 1054. The WCC unanimously defeated an ROC expulsion proposal (Karlsruhe, 2022) and has never addressed Crimea's sovereignty. The Vatican omitted Crimea from the 2016 Havana Declaration, praised "the great Russia of Catherine II" (Aug 2023), and called for "the courage of the white flag" (Feb 2024). Putin declared Crimea "sacred like the Temple Mount" (Dec 2014); Russia then built the New Chersonesos complex, destroying ~85,000 m2 of UNESCO-protected archaeological layers.

## Key findings

1. **Moscow Patriarchate annexed Crimean dioceses.** In June 2022, the ROC unilaterally transferred Dzhankoy, Simferopol, and Feodosiya dioceses from the UOC to a new "Metropolitanate of Crimea." The UOC has never recognized this transfer.
2. **Largest Orthodox schism since 1054.** Moscow broke communion with the Ecumenical Patriarchate on 15 Oct 2018 over Ukrainian autocephaly. The break remains unresolved.
3. **OCU eliminated from Crimea.** Of 46 Kyiv Patriarchate parishes on the peninsula at the time of annexation, zero remain as of 2024. The last OCU church was demolished in June 2024.
4. **WCC: expulsion defeated, Crimea unmentioned.** The ROC is the WCC's single largest member. The 11th Assembly (Karlsruhe, Sept 2022) condemned the invasion as "illegal and unjustifiable" but the expulsion proposal was unanimously defeated. No WCC resolution has addressed Crimea's sovereignty specifically.
5. **Vatican: systematic omission.** The 2016 Havana Declaration (first-ever Pope-Patriarch meeting) deplored "hostility in Ukraine" without naming Crimea. In Aug 2023, Pope Francis praised "the great Russia of Peter the Great, Catherine II." In Feb 2024, he called for "the courage of the white flag."
6. **"Sacred land" claim.** Putin (Dec 4, 2014): "Crimea has sacral meaning for Russia, like the Temple Mount in Jerusalem." This framing links territorial control to religious identity.
7. **New Chersonesos: heritage destruction.** In July 2024, Russia opened the New Chersonesos complex on the UNESCO World Heritage Site (inscribed 2013). ~85,000 m2 of cultural layers destroyed; ~4 million artifacts removed. Built by the Moscow Patriarchate's My History Foundation; Russian Ministry of Defence was general contractor.
8. **Religious registration as sovereignty tool.** Crimean religious communities must add language stating Crimea is part of Russia to their registration documents. Communities that refuse lose legal standing and access to their buildings.
