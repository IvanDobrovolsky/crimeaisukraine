# Travel Platforms — Crimea Sovereignty Representation

**Research date:** 2026-03-30
**Methodology:** Web research, secondary source analysis, platform policy review

---

## 1. Booking.com

**Rating:** ⚠️ Ambiguous / Partially compliant

**Current status:**
- Booking.com **suspended all operations in Russia** since spring 2022, including Crimea
- Prior to 2022, Booking.com had a loophole: searching for Crimea as a "private" trip showed no results, but a general Crimea search returned 5,000+ hotels
- EU sanctions on Crimea (renewed through June 2026) prohibit tourism services to the peninsula

**Historical context:**
- Russia previously considered banning Booking.com over US sanctions compliance ([The Moscow Times, 2018](https://www.themoscowtimes.com/2018/05/23/russia-considers-ban-of-booking-com-over-us-sanctions-a61544))
- Post-February 2022, Booking.com ceased all Russia/Crimea operations

**Country classification:** When Crimea was listed, reports indicate properties appeared under various labels. Current status: no active listings due to sanctions.

**Source:** [PhocusWire: Airbnb and Priceline join Expedia in halting Crimean bookings](https://www.phocuswire.com/Airbnb-and-Priceline-join-Expedia-in-halting-Crimean-travel-bookings-but-others-dont)

## 2. Airbnb

**Rating:** ✅ Correct (compliant with sanctions)

**Current policy:**
- Airbnb **does not permit bookings or listings in Crimea** to comply with international sanctions
- Listings in Crimea have been removed and are "currently not available on the Airbnb platform"
- Listings in the Donbas region were also removed under the same sanctions compliance framework
- Airbnb suspended all operations in Russia and Belarus in 2022

**Disputed territories framework:** In 2018, Airbnb announced a broader "framework for evaluating how the company should treat listings in occupied territories," which it has applied to Crimea, South Ossetia, and Abkhazia.

**Sources:**
- [Airbnb: Actions in response to the Ukraine crisis](https://news.airbnb.com/airbnbs-actions-in-response-to-the-ukraine-crisis/)
- [Airbnb: Listings in Disputed Regions](https://news.airbnb.com/listings-in-disputed-regions/)
- [Airbnb Help: Sanctions impacting hosting in the Donbas region](https://www.airbnb.com/help/article/3209)

## 3. Google Flights / Google Travel

**Rating:** ⚠️ Ambiguous (airport classification)

**Simferopol International Airport (SIP):**
- IATA code: SIP
- ICAO code: UKFF (the "UK" prefix indicates Ukraine)
- Most international aviation databases classify it as **Ukraine**
- Flightradar24 lists it as located in **Ukraine**
- OurAirports states: "most countries in the world do not recognize Russia's annexation of Crimea" and lists it as Ukraine

**Operational status:** All flights to/from SIP have been **suspended since February 24, 2022** due to the Russian invasion. The airport is not currently operational for commercial flights.

**Google Flights:** No flights are bookable to SIP. The airport does not appear in active Google Flights searches since operations ceased.

**Sources:**
- [Flightradar24: SIP Airport](https://www.flightradar24.com/data/airports/SIP)
- [OurAirports: Simferopol](https://ourairports.com/airports/UKFF/)
- [Wikipedia: Simferopol International Airport](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Simferopol_International_Airport)

## 4. Expedia / Hotels.com

**Rating:** ✅ Correct (sanctions-compliant)

**Current status:**
- Expedia Group **ceased all travel sales into and out of Russia** in March 2022 in response to government-imposed sanctions
- Crimea was already blocked prior to 2022 following a US executive order
- Hotels.com (owned by Expedia Group) follows the same policy
- Some Expedia pages may show Crimean hotel listings with "sold out" messaging to prevent bookings

**Sources:**
- [Skift: Expedia Ceases Sales to and From Russia](https://skift.com/2022/03/02/expedia-an-online-travel-standout-ceasing-to-offer-russia-travel/)
- [PhocusWire: Airbnb and Priceline join Expedia in halting Crimean bookings](https://www.phocuswire.com/Airbnb-and-Priceline-join-Expedia-in-halting-Crimean-travel-bookings-but-others-dont)

## 5. TripAdvisor

**Rating:** ⚠️ Ambiguous

**Current representation:**
- TripAdvisor maintains an active destination page for Crimea: [tripadvisor.com/Tourism-g313972-Crimea-Vacations.html](https://www.tripadvisor.com/Tourism-g313972-Crimea-Vacations.html)
- **Crimea is listed under "Europe" without a specific country designation** — the breadcrumb navigation shows: **Europe > Crimea**
- 159,277 reviews of Crimea Hotels, Attractions, and Restaurants remain accessible
- Attractions page also active at [tripadvisor.com/Attractions-g313972-Activities-Crimea.html](https://www.tripadvisor.com/Attractions-g313972-Activities-Crimea.html)

**Assessment:** By listing Crimea directly under "Europe" without a country, TripAdvisor avoids assigning it to either Ukraine or Russia. This is effectively a "disputed" framing that does not align with international law.

## 6. Sanctions Context (2025-2026)

**EU sanctions:** Renewed through **June 23, 2026** — the EU continues to maintain sanctions introduced in response to the illegal annexation of Crimea and Sevastopol.

**US sanctions:** Executive orders prohibiting new investment and trade with Crimea remain in effect.

**Impact:** Major Western travel platforms cannot legally offer services in Crimea regardless of how they classify the territory.

**Source:** [Mayer Brown: Russia/Ukraine Sanctions Update - June 2025](https://www.mayerbrown.com/en/insights/publications/2025/06/russia-ukraine-sanctions-update--month-of-june-2025)
